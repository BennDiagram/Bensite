BEN ORR

STUDENT/MUSICIAN/ARTIST | Pittsburgh, PA

I do a lot of things. I'll tell you about them here.

I'm the guitarist/manager of a popular local funk band called Funky Lamp. I also work with a local organization called Post Genre, where I design visuals, branding, and other marketing materials. I've also been an visual artist all my life, and have done a lot of art for the local music scene.

In my free time, you'll find me cooking, reading, or endlessly scrolling Facebook Marketplace. I found a great deal on a vintage saxophone a few months ago, so I've been learning to play that, too. At one time, I was also a 3D Prop & Environment artist. Lastly, as you can probably see, I mess around with web development.

I'm currently a senior at the University of Pittsburgh, where I'm pursuing a double major in Information Science and Digital Narrative & Interactive Design, alongside certificates in Digital Media and Information System Design. I'm very excited to apply my degrees post-graduation, where I plan to be a server and small-time drug dealer for the forseeable future.

THE BAND

I formed this band with our singer my freshman year, and it's been pretty much my entire life since then. I'm responsible for pretty much everything. I scheduling our practices and book and promote our shows. I design, screenprint, and sell our merchendise. I create all of our online media, which includes promotional material and video content I film with a multi-cam GoPro setup. I maintain our social media presence and website. I also produce and direct the recording of our recorded material, which includes an EP we released in 2022, a single in 2023, and our full-length album that released in March.

RESEARCH

I haven't done any research, but I've tried hard enough that I think this deserves its own section. I was undeclaired and wholly undecided until my second semester of Sophomore year. The following semester, a professor mentioned research during a lecture. I approached him after class and expressed my interest, but he told me that "nobody would want you, you don't have any skills."

Later that semester, I got in touch with another professor expressing interest in an independent study to build the skills I might need to be useful as a research assistant in the School of Computing and Information. He was interested, but due to personal circumstances was on a sabbatical during that semester. These personal circumstances also prevented him from writing back to me without 1-2 follow-up emails, and after about a month of one-sided communication, the add/drop deadline passedk, and I gave up.

I met with another professor that semester in search of advice on research opportunities. He directed me to ANOTHER professor to whom I had emailed earlier that year and never got a response. After a couple emails to this professor, he got back to me, and he invited me to one of his research meetings that summer. While I had to leave early, I sent him an email that evening to follow up on the meeting. He didn't respond. I sent another. He didn't respond. I sent a third, desparate follow-up email, and after not hearing anything back, I gave up on that, too.

INTERNSHIPS

I haven't found any internships either--not for lack of trying. Since last November, I have applied to 72 internships ranging from IT and Data Analysis to Cyber Security, Marketing and PR, Records Management, Event Planning, HR, and everything in between. I've met with four or five separate faculty members for guidance and advice. At their suggestions, I've been to career fairs and resume reviews. I have had two interviews, and, obviously, no offers.
